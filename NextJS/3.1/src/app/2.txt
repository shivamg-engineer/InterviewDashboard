2. List and Explain Three Search Engine Processes
Identify and describe crawling, indexing, and ranking. Use simple language or real-world comparisons.
Expected Outcome:
Clear understanding that crawling is discovery, indexing is storage, and ranking is ordering results.


Ans:- 
Search engines use three main processes to show the best results to users: crawling, indexing, and ranking.

1. Crawling (Discovery)
Crawling is the process where search engines send automated programs called crawlers or bots to discover web pages on the internet. 
These bots move from one page to another by following links, similar to how a person browses different pages by clicking links. 
In simple terms, crawling is like a librarian walking through a library to find new books.

2. Indexing (Storage)
After a page is discovered, the search engine analyzes its content (text, images, keywords, structure) and stores important information in a massive database called the index. 
This step helps the search engine remember what the page is about. It’s similar to a library catalog system where each book is recorded so it can be found quickly later.

3. Ranking (Ordering Results)
When a user searches for something, the search engine looks through its index and decides which pages are the most relevant and useful. 
It then orders these pages in the search results from most relevant to least relevant. This is called ranking. Think of it like a librarian recommending the best books on a topic first.
